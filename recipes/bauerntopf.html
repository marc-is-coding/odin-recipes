Farmer's pot with minced meat, potatoes & paprika - quick family dish

Description

The dish is called "Bauerntopf," a hearty German-style stew made with ground meat, potatoes, and bell peppers. It’s a simple, flavorful recipe using fresh ingredients like firm potatoes, vibrant red, yellow, or orange bell peppers, and high-quality ground beef (often in organic quality). The base is enhanced with onions, garlic, tomato paste, and a mix of aromatic spices including thyme and paprika. The dish is simmered in a savory broth (with an optional splash of red wine or alternatives like balsamic vinegar) until the potatoes are tender and the flavors meld together beautifully. It’s quick to prepare and perfect for a cozy meal that satisfies everyone.

This comforting one-pot meal is versatile and can be customized with additional vegetables like carrots, zucchini, or mushrooms. It’s also easy to adjust for dietary preferences—vegetarian options include plant-based ground meat or lentils. The dish can be slightly thickened for a richer texture using cornstarch, mashed potatoes, or a roux. Suitable for meal prep, it stores well for a couple of days, making it a great option for busy families or weeknight dinners. With its robust flavor and ease of preparation, Bauerntopf is a beloved comfort food that’s sure to please.

Ingredients

  • potatoes
  • bell peppers (red, yellow, or orange)
  • onion
  • olive oil
  • ground beef
  • red wine (optional)
  • tomato paste
  • garlic
  • beef broth
  • thyme
  • sweet paprika powder
  • hot paprika powder
  • smoked paprika powder (optional)
  • salt and pepper
  • cornstarch or sauce thickener (if needed)
  • parsley
  • cornstarch or sauce thickener (if needed) parsley

Steps

  1. Peel, wash, and dice the potatoes into bite-sized pieces. Wash, deseed, and dice the bell peppers. Peel and finely chop the onion.
  2. Heat a bit of olive oil in a large pot and crumble the ground beef into the pot. Cook until browned.
  3. Add the chopped onions, minced garlic, and tomato paste to the pot. Stir and sauté briefly.
  4. Deglaze the pot with red wine (optional) and let it reduce slightly.
  5. Add the diced potatoes and bell peppers to the pot. Stir and cook for a few minutes.
  6. Prepare the broth by dissolving instant beef or vegetable stock in 500 ml of boiling water. Pour the broth into the pot.
  7. Add thyme, sweet paprika powder, hot paprika powder, smoked paprika powder (optional), salt, and pepper. Stir well to combine all ingredients.
  8. Let the stew simmer for about 20 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender. Stir occasionally during cooking.
  9. If the stew is too liquid, thicken it by mixing a tablespoon of cornstarch with cold water, then adding it to the pot while stirring continuously.
  10. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.
  11. Sprinkle freshly chopped parsley over the stew before serving.
  12. Serve hot and enjoy your Bauerntopf!
