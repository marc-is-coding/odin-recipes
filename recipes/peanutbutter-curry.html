Peanutbutter-Curry

Description

This meal is a simple and flavorful vegetable curry made with fresh or frozen vegetables, curry paste, coconut milk, and a spoonful of peanut butter. It’s incredibly easy to prepare, even for beginners, and delivers bold flavors with minimal effort. The dish is highly flexible, allowing you to use any vegetables you have on hand, such as broccoli, zucchini, carrots, or bell peppers. The vegetables are cooked briefly to retain their crunchiness, and the curry is topped with optional garnishes like yogurt, fresh cilantro, and nuts for added flavor.

Served with jasmine or basmati rice, this peanut butter curry is perfect for a quick weeknight dinner or meal prep, as it often leaves leftovers for the next day. The recipe is part of the author’s cookbook "4-Week Weeknight Cooking," which focuses on quick, uncomplicated meals for busy evenings. It’s a versatile, satisfying dish that can be easily adapted to suit personal tastes.

Ingredients

  • peanut oil
  • carrots
  • red bell peppers
  • broccoli
  • zucchini
  • snow peas
  • spring onions
  • ginger
  • cherry tomatoes
  • peanut butter
  • curry paste
  • coconut milk
  • cilantro
  • peanuts
  • yogurt

Steps

  1. Wash all the vegetables thoroughly and cut them into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Peel and grate or finely chop the ginger. Remove the string from the snow peas if necessary.
  3. Heat the oil in a pan over medium heat.
  4. Start by sautéing the carrots for a couple of minutes, then add the red bell peppers and broccoli.
  5. After a few minutes, add the zucchini and cherry tomatoes to the pan.
  6. Add the grated ginger, peanut butter, curry paste, and coconut milk to the pan. Stir everything well until combined.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Cook the curry for a few more minutes, ensuring the vegetables remain slightly crunchy.
  9. Serve the curry with rice on the side.
  10. Top with fresh coriander, peanuts, or a dollop of yogurt if desired.
